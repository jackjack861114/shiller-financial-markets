# Financial Markets (2011), ECON 252 Lecture 17｜Yale ECON252（2011）第17講：選擇權市場｜中英對照稿

## Chapter 1. Examples of Options Markets and Core Terms｜第一章：選擇權市場示例與核心術語

**Professor Robert Shiller｜Robert Shiller 教授：**

All right. Good morning. The subject of today's lecture is options. And I think, maybe I'd better first define what an option is, before I move to say anything about them. Because some of you may not have encountered them, because they're not part of everyday life for most people, although they are, in a sense. I'll get back to that. Let me just define terms here.

各位早。今天這講的主題是選擇權（options）。我想最好先定義什麼是選擇權，再談其他。因為在座有些人可能還沒接觸過——對多數人而言，選擇權並非日常生活的一部分，儘管在某種意義上其實是。我稍後會再回來講這點。先把名詞定清楚。

So there's two kinds of options. There's a call and a put, OK? A call option is an option to buy something at a specified price, and the price is called the "exercise price" or "strike price." Those are synonyms.

選擇權有兩種：買權（call）與賣權（put）。買權是在約定價格買進某物的權利，該價格稱為 exercise price 或 strike price，兩詞同義。

And a put option is the right to sell something at the specified exercise price. And it has another term it has to be specified, and that's the exercise date. The exercise date applies to both call and put options. OK.

賣權則是在約定的履約價賣出某物的權利。還必須指定另一項：履約日（exercise date）。履約日對買權與賣權皆適用。

Options go back thousands of years. It must have happened before we have any recorded records. If you're thinking of buying something from someone, but you don't want to put up the money today, you go to some lawyer, and say, write up a contract. I want to buy an option to buy this thing.

選擇權可追溯到數千年前，想必在有文字紀錄之前就已存在。若你想向某人買東西、但不想今天就拿出全部現金，你可以找律師說：幫我寫一份合約，我要買一個「未來可買進該物」的選擇權。

So, for example, if you are thinking of building a building on land that is owned now by a farmer, but you're not ready to do it. You may be thinking about it. You can go to the farmer and say, I'd like to buy that corner of that acre there. I'd like to have the right to buy it. I'll pay you now for the right. And you get a lawyer and you write up a contract. And that's an option. You have an option to buy at the exercise price until the exercise date.

例如，你想在農民擁有的土地上蓋建物，但尚未準備好動工，仍在評估。你可以找農民說：我想買那一角那一英畝的「買進權」；我現在就付錢換取這個權利。請律師寫約，那就是選擇權：你在履約日之前有權以履約價買下該地。

Now, in modern terminology, we have two kinds of options, American and European. It doesn't refer to geography, those two terms. The terms refer, instead, to when you can exercise.

現代用語裡還有兩種：美式（American）與歐式（European），與地理無關，而是指「何時可以履約」。

So, the American option is better than the European option for the buyer, because the American option can be exercised at any time until the exercise date. Whereas the European option can be exercised only on the exercise date. But you see the American option has to be better, or not worse than—I don't know if it's strictly better—but not worse than the European option because you have more options. The latter "options" is used in the sense of flexibility.

對買方而言，美式選擇權優於（至少不劣於）歐式，因為美式可在到期日前任何時點履約，歐式則只能在到期當日履約。美式不會更差，因為你擁有更大的彈性（此處 options 指「選擇餘地」）。

I think, we've defined what they are. Do you understand well enough what they are? They occur naturally in life. I remember, Avinash Dixit was writing about options and he said, well, when you're dating someone and you know the person will marry you, you have an option that you can exercise at any time by agreeing to marry.

我想定義到這裡夠了。大家是否清楚？它們在生活中自然出現。我記得 Avinash Dixit 寫到選擇權時說：若你與某人交往且確知對方願意嫁／娶你，你就擁有一個選擇權，隨時可透過同意結婚來履約。

Now, one of the theorems in option theory is, you usually don't want to exercise a call option early. This theorem abstracts from dividend-payments of the underlying asset. And so, Dixit would say, well, maybe that's why a lot of people have trouble getting married. They don't want to exercise their option early.

選擇權理論有一條定理：你通常不會想提早履約買權（此處抽象掉標的資產發放股利的情況）。於是 Dixit 會說：也許這就是許多人遲遲不婚的原因——他們不想太早履約。

What we'll see is that options have option value. They give you a choice, and so there's something there. When you exercise an option, that is, when you actually buy the thing, or in the case of a put, sell the thing, then you're losing the choice. So, you've given up something. Of course, you have to also exercise eventually, if things are going to make sense.

我們會看到選擇權具有「選擇權價值」（option value）：它給你選擇，因此有其價值。當你履約——買權是實際買進、賣權是實際賣出——你就放棄了選擇，等於放棄了某些東西。當然，若一切要合理，最終仍可能必須履約。

Usually, when we talk about options, we're talking now about options to buy a share of stock, or 100 shares of stock, and that's the usual example. But they occur all over the place.

通常我們談的是買進一股或一百股股票的選擇權，這是最常見的例子，但它們無所不在。

Let me mention some other examples of options. The usual story is the stock option. You go to your broker and you say, I'd like to buy an option to buy 100 shares of Microsoft. I don't want to buy Microsoft, I want to buy an option to buy Microsoft, which happens to be cheaper, by the way, usually. Usually, it costs more money to actually buy the thing than to buy the right to buy the thing at another price. We'll get back to that.

再舉些例子。典型故事是股票選擇權：你對經紀人說，我想買買進一百股 Microsoft 的選擇權；我不想直接買 Microsoft，而是買「未來可買」的權利——而且通常這比較便宜。一般而言，直接買標的比買「以另一價格買進的權利」花更多錢。這點我們稍後再談。

But in a sense, let's think about this, stocks themselves are options in a sense, with a zero exercise price. Maybe, I'll have to get back and explain, but what I mean is—let me get back and explain that in a minute.

某種意義上，股票本身也像選擇權，履約價為零。我可能晚點再解釋——讓我過一會兒再說明。

But let me go ahead to other examples, mortgages. An ordinary home mortgage has an option characteristic to it, in the sense that, if the price of your home drops a lot, you can just walk away from the mortgage, and say, I'm out of here. It's like not exercising an option. It's analogous. Or I can choose to prepay a mortgage early, and that's like exercising an option. So, option pricing gets into all sorts of things. OK.

先看其他例子：房貸。一般住宅抵押貸款帶有選擇權色彩：若房價大跌，你可以違約走人，像是不履約；或你可選擇提前清償，那就像履約。因此選擇權定價進入各種領域。

## Chapter 2. Purposes of Option Contracts｜第二章：選擇權合約的目的

I thought I should say something about the purposes of options, before I move on to try to discuss what their properties and pricing are, which is the main subject of this lecture. I can give two different justifications for options.

在進入主題——性質與定價——之前，我想先談選擇權的目的。我可以用兩套理由來說明。

Why do we have options? Some people cynically think that options are just gambling vehicles. It's another way to gamble. You can go to the casino, you can play poker, or you can buy options. Well, I think for some people that's just what it is, they're volatile risky investments that can make you a lot of money.

為何要有選擇權？有人犬儒地認為那只是賭博工具：去賭場、打撲克，或買選擇權。對某些人而言確實如此——高波動、高風險，也可能大賺。

But I think, they have a basic purpose, or purposes. First of all, theoretical. If we were trying to design the ideal financial system, what would we do? Some people thought of ideal economic systems without reference to finance, like Karl Marx—I come back to him—the great communist, thought that we would have an ideal communist state and there'd be no financial markets. When they actually tried it and they tried to do it, I think they gradually realized that not having any financial markets makes our entrepreneurship, our management of enterprises, kind of blind. We can't see where we're going because there's no prices. We don't know what anything is worth. There was an old joke that the communist countries survived only because they had prices from capitalist countries to rely on. Otherwise, they don't know anything about values or profits, right?

但我認為它有根本目的。首先是理論面：若我們設計理想的金融體系會怎麼做？有人想像不含金融的理想經濟，例如 Karl Marx——我之後還會提到——這位共產主義大師認為理想國度裡不該有金融市場。真正嘗試後，他們逐漸發現：沒有金融市場會讓創業與企業經營變得盲目；沒有價格就看不見方向，不知道任何東西值多少。老笑話說：共產國家能撐下去只因還能依賴資本主義國家的價格，否則價值與利潤都成謎。

So, we need prices. Many people have written about this, but I mentioned, in 1964, Kenneth Arrow, who is an economic theorist, wrote a classic paper, in which he argued that, unless we have prices for all states of nature, there's a sense in which the economic system is inefficient. You really need the price of everything, including the price of some possibility. In a sense, that's what options are giving you, the existence of options is giving you.

我們需要價格。很多人寫過，我這裡提 1964 年 Kenneth Arrow 的經典論文：若沒有對「所有自然狀態」定價，經濟體系在某種意義上是無效率的；你需要萬物的價格，包含某些可能性的價格。選擇權的存在，正是在提供這件事。

So, Steven Ross, who used to teach here at Yale, a friend of mine, lives here in New Haven, in 1976, in The Quarterly Journal of Economics, wrote a classic paper about options, showing that, in a sense, they complete the state space. They create prices for everything that affects decision-making. I'm not going to get into the technicalities of the paper, but I wanted to start with a theoretical justification for options, so you'll see why we're doing this.

Steven Ross 曾在此任教，是我朋友，住 New Haven；1976 年在 *The Quarterly Journal of Economics* 發表關於選擇權的經典論文，指出它們在某種意義上「完備狀態空間」——為影響決策的一切創造價格。我不會深入技術細節，但先給理論正當性，你們才知道為何要學。

I don't want this to come across as a lecture, how you can gamble in the options market. This is about making things work right for the economic system, improving human welfare. But a lot of people don't get that. That's why Karl Marx was so successful. It seems too abstract. What does this options market do for us?

我不希望這講變成「教你如何在選擇權市場賭博」。這是關於讓經濟體系運作得更好、增進福祉。但很多人聽不懂——那也是 Marx 能成功的原因之一：太抽象。選擇權市場到底為我們做什麼？

Let me just go back to the example I started out with. You're a construction firm, and you're thinking of building something, a new supermarket, where people can buy their food. And you note that there's a pair of expressways crossing somewhere, and you think, that's the perfect place to build a mega supermarket, because everyone can get there by car. And there's a lot of land, I can build a big parking lot, perfect. But before you think further, you go to buy an option on the land, right? So, you knock on the door at the farmhouse, and there's a farmer with all these acres, and you say, I'm thinking of building a mega supermarket here. I'd like to buy an option on your farm.

回到開頭例子。你是建商，想在兩條高速公路交會處蓋大型超市——大家開車都方便，空地大可停車，完美。但在深想之前，你先去買土地的選擇權。你敲農舍的門，對擁有大片土地的農民說：我想在這裡蓋大型超市，想買你農場的選擇權。

You learn something at that moment. You might learn that the farmer says, I've already sold an option, so I can't do it. You could try to talk to the person I sold it to, and see if you can buy it from him. Or the farmer might say, I've had three other offers, and I'm raising my price to some millions of millions of dollars. Then, you have second thoughts about doing it.

當下你就學到資訊。農民可能說：我已經賣出選擇權了，沒辦法。你得去找買方看能否轉買。或者農民說：還有三組人出價，我要把價格抬到天文數字。你就會重新考慮。

You see what I'm saying, that the price discovery is in there? It's making things happen differently. You're learning something. The farmer is learning something. You are learning something from the options market, and ultimately it decides where that supermarket will go. So, that's the theoretical purpose of option.

你看出「價格發現」在裡面了嗎？它改變事情如何發生。你學到、農民學到，選擇權市場傳遞資訊，最終決定超市落點。這就是選擇權的理論目的。

I wanted to talk, also, about a behavioral purpose of options. It's a little fuzzier about the actual benefits of options from this standpoint. The behavioral theory of options says that—very many different aspects of human behavior tie into options, but I would say it has something to do with attention anomalies and salience.

再談行為面的目的，效益較難精確衡量。行為理論認為：人類行為很多面向都與選擇權有關，我會說它牽涉「注意力異常」（attention anomalies）與顯著性（salience）。

Psychologists talk about this, that people make mistakes very commonly in what they pay attention to, what strikes the fancy of their imagination. Salience is something psychologists also talk about. Salient events are events that tend to attract attention, tend to be remembered.

心理學家說，人常搞錯該注意什麼、什麼會抓住想像。顯著事件較易吸引注意、被記住。

Now, when you think of options, a lot of options are what are called incentive options, OK? And when you get your first job, you may discover this. They'll give you options to buy shares in the company you work for. Why do they do that? I think it's because of certain human behavioral traits that I mention here, your attention and your salience.

許多選擇權是所謂激勵選擇權（incentive options）。你第一份工作可能就遇到：公司給你買自家股票的選擇權。為什麼？我認為與我剛提的行為特質有關：注意力與顯著性。

It's not necessarily very expensive for a company to give you options to buy shares in the company, but it puts you in a situation, where you start to pay attention to the value of the company. It becomes salient for you, and you start hoping that the price of the company will go up, because you have options to buy it, at a strike price. You hope that the company's price per share goes above your strike price, because then your options are worth something. They're in the money. So, it may change your motivation and your morale at work, or sense of identity with the company. All these sorts of things figure in. That's why we have incentive options. They can also give you peace of mind.

公司發給你買股選擇權未必很貴，但會讓你開始注意公司價值；它對你變得顯著，你會希望股價上漲，因為你有以履約價買進的權利；你希望股價超過履約價，選擇權才有價值（in the money）。這可能改變工作動機、士氣或對公司的認同。因此有激勵選擇權，也能帶來心安。

Insurance is actually related to options in the sense that, when you buy insurance on your house, it's like buying a put option on your house, although it may be not directly connected to the home's value, right? When you buy an insurance policy in your house, and the house burns down, you collect on the insurance policy. Well, the price of your house fell to zero. If you had bought a put option on the house, it would do the same thing, right? You would have an option to sell it at a high price on something that's now worthless. So, insurance is like options, and insurance gives you peace of mind.

保險與選擇權也有關：買房屋保險像買房屋的賣權，雖未必與房價一一連動。房子燒毀你領保險金，等於房價歸零；若你買了賣權，也能在標的一文不值時以高價賣出的權利來對沖。保險像選擇權，也給人心安。

So, people think in certain repetitive patterns, and one of them is, that I would like to not worry about something. So, I can get peace of mind, if I have a put option on something that I might otherwise worry about. All right, maybe that's enough of an introduction, but I've given you both theoretical reasons for options and behavioral reasons. I think of them as basically inevitable. You may have people advising you not to bother with options markets. That might be right for you, in a sense, but I think that they're always going to be with us, and so it's something that we have to understand.

人們有重複的思維模式，其中之一是「我不想為某事煩惱」；若對擔憂之物持有賣權，可獲心安。介紹先到這裡；我已給理論與行為兩套理由。我認為選擇權基本上不可避免。也許有人勸你別碰選擇權市場——對你也許是對的——但它們會一直存在，我們必須理解。

## Chapter 3. Quoted Prices of Options and the Role of Derivatives Markets｜第三章：選擇權報價與衍生性市場的角色

I have a newspaper clipping that I cut out. I've been teaching this course for over 20 years, so sometimes I don't update my newspaper clippings. I have a newspaper clipping from the options page that I made in 2002, OK? So, that's nine years ago. But I can't update it anymore, because newspapers don't print option prices anymore. So, I could go on some electronic trade account and get an updated option page. But why don't we just stick with The Wall Street Journal? This is a clipping from The Wall Street Journal, April 2002, when they used to have an options page, OK?

我有一份剪報。我教這門課超過二十年，有時來不及更新剪報。這份是 2002 年的選擇權版面——距今九年。現在報紙已不印選擇權報價，我無法再更新；本可上電子交易帳戶看最新版面，但我們就沿用《華爾街日報》（The Wall Street Journal）吧：2002 年四月，當時還有選擇權專頁。

I just picked America Online. I don't know why. It's an interesting company. You remember America Online, a web presence? It used to be bigger than it is now. And actually, in 2000, America Online merged with Time Warner, OK? So, we actually have two different rows corresponding—Forget Ace Limited, the second row says AOL.TW. That's America Online Time Warner, the merged company, and then below that, they have America Online itself. These were options that were issued before the merger, and they apparently are being exercised in terms of the same AOL Time Warner stock.

我隨便挑了 America Online（AOL），一家有趣的公司。你們記得 AOL 這個網路品牌嗎？它曾比現在更大。2000 年 AOL 與 Time Warner 合併。表上有兩列——別管 Ace Limited——第二列是 AOL.TW，即合併後的 America Online Time Warner；下面還有 America Online 本身。這些是合併前發行的選擇權，履約時似乎仍以同一 AOL Time Warner 股票結算。

AOL, by the way, was spun off by Time Warner last year, so they had a divorce. They were married in 2000. They were divorced in 2010. So you can get back to it, AOL options, now.

順帶一提，AOL 去年由 Time Warner 分拆，等於離婚：2000 年結婚、2010 年離婚。現在又可單獨談 AOL 選擇權。

So, anyway, it shows the price of the share at $21.85 a share. So, you take any of these rows, and it shows you, for various strike prices, what the options prices are. So, let's go to the top row. A strike price of 20, expiring in May of 2002, which is one month into the future. Remember, it's April 2002 right now. The volume is the number of options that were traded yesterday, and the $2.55 up there is the price of a call option, the last price of the option to be traded yesterday. This is the morning paper. It's reporting on yesterday's prices at closing. And then, there's put options traded. A lot more puts were traded on that day. There were 2000 put options traded on that day in April 2002, and the last price of the put option was $0.85. For $0.85, you could buy the right to sell a share of AOL Time Warner at 20, OK? And similarly, you could buy the right to buy it at $20.00 for $2.55. So, these are different strike prices and different exercise dates. 要消去風險，選 \(H\) 使兩種情況下期價值相等，得到無風險投組。令兩式相等解 \(H\)，可得 \(H = (C_u - C_d)/((u-d)S)\)（講堂口述分母偶有括號不清；標準寫法如上）。

So, I've been able to put together a portfolio of the stock and the option that has zero risk. If I do this, if I hold this amount of shares in my portfolio, I've got a riskless portfolio. So, that means that the riskless portfolio has to earn the riskless rate, right? It's the same thing as a riskless investment, so it has to earn the riskless rate. If I can erase this now, I'm almost there, through option pricing.

如此得到零風險投組；無風險投組的報酬率必須等於無風險利率 \(r\)（講者口語曾說成「與無風險利率同一回事」——意指應賺無風險報酬）。快到選擇權定價了。

The option pricing then says that, since I've derived what H is, the portfolio has to be worth (1+r) times what I put in, which is HS minus C. And that has to equal the value of the portfolio at the end, which is either uHS minus Cu, or dHS minus Cd, the same thing, OK?

因此 \((1+r)\times\)（期初投入）\(=\) 期末價值。期初投入為「買進 \(H\) 股成本 \(HS\) 減去賣買權收到的權利金 \(C\)」；期末在兩種狀態下應相等，皆為 \(uHS - C_u\)（亦等於 \(dHS - C_d\)）。

So, I've already derived what H is, and I substituted into that, and I solved for C. So, substitute H in and solve for C, and we get the call price, OK? It's a little bit complicated, but with risk-neutral probability \(p = \dfrac{1+r-d}{u-d}\), one standard compact form is \(C = \dfrac{1}{1+r}\,\bigl(p\,C_u + (1-p)\,C_d\bigr)\), equivalent to the boxed expression the instructor wrote on the board with the same \(p\) and \((1-p)\) weights.

代入 \(H\) 解出 \(C\)。講者板書形式可整理為：令 \(p=(1+r-d)/(u-d)\)，則 \(C=\bigl(p C_u + (1-p)C_d\bigr)/(1+r)\)，與他寫的「兩項加權再折現」一致。

And I'll put a box around that because that's our option price formula, OK? Did you follow all that?

他會在這式子外畫框——這就是我們的選擇權定價公式。大家跟上了嗎？

This is derived—this option price formula was derived from a no arbitrage condition. Arbitrage, in finance, means riskless profit opportunity. And the no arbitrage condition says, it's never possible to make more than the riskless rate risklessly, all right?

此式來自**無套利**（no arbitrage）條件。套利指無風險獲利機會；無套利指：不可能無風險地賺超過無風險利率。

If I could, suppose I had some way—suppose the riskless rate is 5%, and I can make 6% risklessly, then I will borrow at the riskless rate and put it into the 6% opportunity. And I'll do that till kingdom come. There's no limit to how much I'll do that. I'll do it forever. It's too much of a profit opportunity to ever happen. One of the most powerful insights of theoretical finance is, that the no arbitrage condition should hold.

若無風險利率 5% 卻能無風險賺 6%，我會借 5% 投入 6%，無限放大。這種機會不該長期存在。理論金融最強的洞見之一，就是無套利應成立。

It's like saying, there are no $10 bills on the pavement. When you walk down the street and you see a $10 bill lying there on the street, your first thought ought to be, are my eyes deceiving me? Because somebody else would have picked it up if it were there. How can it be there?

就像說人行道上不會躺著十元鈔——若看見，先懷疑眼睛；真有的話早被人撿走。

I once actually had that experience. I was walking down the street in New York. It was actually a $5 bill. It was just lying there in the street. And so, I reached down to pick it up, and then, suddenly, it disappeared. And it was people on one of the stoops of one of these New York townhouses playing a game. They'd tied a string to a $5 bill. And they would leave it on the street, and watch people reach for it, and they'd snatch it away. That's the only time in my life I ever saw a $5 bill on the pavement. 我真的遇過一次：在 New York 街上看到五元鈔，一撿就消失——是聯排屋門階上的人玩遊戲，鈔票綁了線。那是我唯一一次在人行道看到鈔票。所以假設「看到也不是真的」很合理。

And that's all this is saying, that if the option price didn't follow this formula, something would be wrong. And so, it had better follow this formula. Now, that is the basic core option theory.

同理，若選擇權價格不符此式，就有哪裡不對；它最好符合。這就是選擇權理論的核心。

Now, the interesting thing about this theory is, I didn't use the probability of up and the probability of down. 有趣的是：推導完全沒用到「上漲機率／下跌機率」。有人會說：我買選擇權不就是賭它會進價內嗎？剛剛 0.45 美元那例，我說很便宜因為大概漲不到 25——聽起來像機率。但此式裡根本沒有機率。我沒有唬你：你不必知道進價內的機率，也能用純無套利定價。

## Chapter 7. The Black-Scholes Option Pricing Formula｜第七章：Black-Scholes 選擇權定價公式

And it caused fear, and it caused a big spike up in volatility of the stock market, but not quite as big as the current financial crisis.

圖上也標出 1974 第一次石油危機——我們課堂談過：當時油價曾被 Texas Railroad Commission 的穩定機制鎖住；機制瓦解、OPEC 首次展現力量，形成新的現實感，引發恐懼與股市波動尖峰，但仍略小於這次金融危機。

So, this is an interesting chart to me. A lot of things I learned from this chart, and let me conclude with some thoughts about this. But what I learned from this chart is that, somehow, financial markets are very stable for a long time.

這張圖對我很有啟發。我從中學到：金融市場在很長時間內往往相當穩定。

So, it would seem like it wouldn't be that much of an extrapolation—when are you people going to retire? You've pick a retirement date yet? Well, let's say a half century from now, ok? So, that would be 2060? So, you're going to retire out here, all right? Your whole life is in here. What do you think volatility is going to do over that whole period?

因此，把走勢再外推半個世紀似乎也不算太離譜——你們打算何時退休？選好日期了嗎？假設再過五十年，大約 2060？你們會落在圖的更右側才退休，對吧？你們的整段人生大概都在這個區間裡。你覺得那段期間波動會怎麼走？

Well, judging from the plot, it's probably pretty similar, right? That's not much more history compared to what we've already seen. Probably just going to keep doing this. But there's this risk of something like this happening again. And we saw a near miss here, but this plot encourages me to think that maybe outliers, or fat tails, or black swan events, are the big disruptors to economic theory.

從圖看，可能還是差不多——相對於已觀察到的歷史，再過半世紀不算更長。但也可能再出現像大蕭條那樣的段。我們剛經歷一次擦身而過。這張圖讓我更相信：**離群、肥尾、黑天鵝**才是打斷經濟理論的大干擾。

Black-Scholes is not a black swan theory. It assumes normality of distributions, and so, it's not always reliable. So, this leads me to think that option pricing theory—I presented a theory. The Black-Scholes theory is very elegant and a very useful tool, especially useful when things behave normally. But, I think, one always has to keep in the back of one's mind, the risk of sudden major changes like we've seen here.

Black-Scholes 不是黑天鵝理論；它假設常態性，故不永遠可靠。選擇權定價理論——我呈現了一套——Black-Scholes 優雅且實用，尤其在「近似常態」時。但你心裡要留一塊位置給突發、巨大的結構變化風險。

## Chapter 9. The Potential for Options in the Housing Market｜第九章：住宅市場中的選擇權潛力

So, let me just you give us some final thoughts about options. I launched this lecture by saying, they're very important. And they affect our lives in many ways. I've been trying to campaign for the expansion of our financial markets.

最後再談選擇權。開場我說它很重要，影響生活很多面向。我一直倡議擴張金融市場工具。

Working with my colleagues and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange, we launched options, in 2006, on single-family homes in the United States. We were hoping that people would buy put options to protect themselves against home price declines, but the market never took off. We have, since, seen huge human suffering because of the failure of people to protect themselves against home price declines.

我與同事及 Chicago Mercantile Exchange 在 2006 年推出美國獨棟住宅的選擇權，希望人們買賣權對沖房价下跌，但市場沒做起來。後來我們看到人們未能保護自己免於房价下跌，造成巨大痛苦。

There were various noises that were made by people in power, that suggested that maybe something could be done. President Obama proposed something called Home Price Protection Program, and it sounded like an option, a put option program. But, actually, it was a much more subtle program than that. It was a program to incentivize mortgage originators to do workouts on mortgages, if the mortgages would default—if home prices were to fall. And nothing really happened with it. The President can't get things started, either, always.

當權者曾釋出訊號想做事。Obama 總統提過 Home Price Protection Program，聽像賣權／房价保護，但其實更細：想激勵房貸發放者在房价下跌、違約風險上升時做債務重整（workouts）。最後沒什麼成案——總統也不總能推動事情。

I've been proposing that mortgages should have put options on the house attached to them. When you buy a house, get a mortgage, you should automatically get a put option. I've got a new paper on that.

我主張房貸應附帶房屋的賣權：你買房辦貸款時，應自動取得賣權。我有一篇新論文在談這個。

But these are kind of futuristic things at the moment. I'm just saying this at the end, just to try to impress on you, what I think is the real importance of options markets. People don't manage risks well in the present world. Having options or insurance-like contracts of an expanded nature will help people manage their risks better, and it will make for a better world.

這些此刻仍偏未來式。我在結尾說這些，是想讓你們感受選擇權市場真正的重量：現世人們風險管理不好；更多選擇權或類保險合約，能幫人更好管理風險，世界也會更好。

OK. I'll see you on Monday.

好，週一見。
