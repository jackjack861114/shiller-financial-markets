## Chapter 2. Introduction to Probability Theory｜第 2 章：機率論導論

But basically, let me just try to elaborate on—probability theory is a conceptual framework that mathematicians invented. And it's become a very important way of thinking, but it doesn't go back that far in time. The word probability in its present meaning wasn't even coined until the 1600's. So, you if you talk to someone before the year 1600, and say, this has a probability of 0.5, they would have no idea what you're talking about. So, it's a major advance in human understanding to think in terms of probabilities. Now we do. And now it's routine, but it wasn't routine at all. And part of what I'm thinking about is, what probability theorists do, or in particular finance theorists like to do, is they think that the world is, well, let me just say, it's kind of a realization that the world is very complex, and that the outcomes that we see are the results of millions of little things. And the stories we tell are just stories.

基本上，讓我把機率論講清楚一點：它是數學家發明的一套概念架構，已成為非常重要的思考方式，但歷史其實沒那麼久。probability 這個詞在現在意義上要到 17 世紀才被造出來。所以若你在 1600 年以前對人說「這件事機率是 0.5」，對方完全不懂你在說什麼。用人類理解來說，能用機率思考是一大躍進。現在我們覺得理所當然，但以前完全不是。我常想：機率論者、尤其是金融理論家在做什麼？他們意識到世界非常複雜，我們看到的結果是千千萬萬小事的總和。而我們講的故事，就只是故事。

So, how do we deal with the complexity of the world? Well, we do it by dealing with all of these little incremental shocks that affect our lives in a mathematical way. And we think of them as millions of shocks. How do they accumulate? We have mathematical laws of how they accumulate. And once we understand those laws, we can build mathematical models of the outcomes. And then we can ask whether we should be surprised by the financial events that we've seen. It's a little bit like science, real hard science.

那我們如何處理世界的複雜性？做法是用數學方式處理那些影響我們生活的小增量衝擊。我們把它們想成數以百萬計的衝擊。它們如何累積？我們有描述累積方式的數學定律。一旦理解那些定律，就能建立結果的數學模型，然後問：我們見到的金融事件是否該令人驚訝？這有點像真正的硬科學。

## Chapter 3. Financial Return and Basic Statistical Concepts｜第 3 章：金融報酬與基本統計概念

I want to start out with just the concept of return. Which is, in finance, the basic, the most basic concept that—

我想從「報酬」（return）這個概念開始。在金融裡，它是最基本、最核心的概念——

**Professor Robert Shiller｜Robert Shiller 教授：**

When you invest in something, you have to do it for a time interval. And I'm writing the return as one time period. T is time. And so, it could be a year, or it could be months, or it could be a day. We're going to number these months, let's say it's monthly return, we're going to number these months, so that the first month is number one, second month is number two.

當你投資某個東西，一定跨越一段時間區間。我把報酬寫成一個時間期間。T 代表時間；可以是一年、幾個月，或一天。我們把月份編號——假設是月報酬，第一個月叫 1，第二個月叫 2。

And so, return at time t, if t is equal to 3, that would be the return at month three. And we'll do price at the beginning of the month. And so, what is your return to investing in something? It's the increase in the price. That's p_{t+1} − p_t. I'm spelling it out here. Price—I spelled it out in the numerator, I guess I didn't do it in the denominator. It's price at time t+1 minus the price at time t, which is called the capital gain, plus the dividend, which is a check you receive, if you do, from the company that you're investing in. That's the return.

所以在時間 t 的報酬，若 t 等於 3，就是第三個月的報酬。我們用月初價格。投資某物的報酬是什麼？是價格的增加：p_{t+1} − p_t。我在這裡寫清楚：分子是價格——分母我沒寫得很完整——是 t+1 時點價格減 t 時點價格，稱為資本利得（capital gain），再加上股利（dividend），也就是你投資的公司若發放、你會收到的支票。這就是報酬。

We have something else called gross return. Which is just 1 plus the return. Returns can be positive or negative. They can never be more than—never be less than minus 100%. In a limited liability economy that we live in, the law says that you cannot lose more than the money you put in, and that's going to be our assumption. So, return is between minus 100% and plus infinity. And gross return is always positive. It's between zero and infinity.

我們還有另一個量叫總報酬（gross return），就是 1 加上報酬。報酬可正可負，但不會低於 −100%——在我們生活的有限責任經濟裡，法律上你不能賠超過你投入的本金，這是我們的假設。所以報酬介於 −100% 到正無窮；總報酬永遠為正，介於 0 到正無窮。

Now what we're going to do—this is the primary thing that we want to study, because we are interested in investing and in making a return. So, we want to do some evaluations of the success of an investment. So, I want to now talk about some basic statistical concepts that we can apply to returns and to other random variables as well. These on this slide are mostly concepts that you've already heard. This is expected value. This is the mathematical expectation of a random variable x, which could be the return, or the gross return, or something else, but we're going to substitute something else.

接下來這是我們主要要研究的，因為我們關心投資與賺取報酬。我們要評估一筆投資是否成功。所以現在要談一些基本統計概念，可用在報酬上，也可用在其它的隨機變數上。投影片上多數是你們聽過的概念。這是期望值（expected value），隨機變數 x 的數學期望；x 可以是報酬、總報酬或其它東西——我們會代入具體內容。

We're going to substitute in what they are. So, the expectation of x, or the mean of x, μ_x is another term for it, is the weighted sum of all possible values of x weighted by their probabilities. And the probabilities have to sum to 1. They're positive numbers, or zero or positive numbers, reflecting the likelihood of that random variable occurring, that value of the random variable occurring.

我們把它們代進去。x 的期望值，或 x 的平均數，另一個寫法是 μ_x，是所有可能 x 值以其機率加權的加總。機率必須加總為 1；它們是非負數，反映該隨機變數取到該值的相對可能性。

So, I have here—there's an infinite number of possible values for x, and we have a probability for each one, and the expectation of x is that weighted sum of those, weighted by probabilities, of those possible values. This is for a discrete random variable that takes on only a finite, only a countable number of values. If it's a continuous random variable, if x is continuous, then the expectation of x is an integral of the probability density of x, times x dx. I'm just writing that down for you now for completeness. But I'm not going to explain or elaborate that.

這裡 x 的可能值可以無窮多個，每個值有一個機率；期望值就是那些可能值以機率加權的和。這是離散隨機變數（只取有限或可數無窮多個值）的情況。若 x 連續，期望值是機率密度乘上 x 再對 x 積分。我為了完整性寫下來，但不會在此細講。

These two formulas here are measures of the central tendency of x, OK. It's essentially the average of x in the probability metric that we have up here. And so, that's a formula that's used to estimate the average return of a portfolio of investments, where we use gross return for x, not just the simple return. If you put in a negative value, you might get a negative product, and then, if you took the nth root of that, it could be an imaginary number, so let's forget that. We're not going to apply this formula if there are any negative numbers. But it's often used, and I recommend its use, in evaluating investments. Because if you use gross return, it gives a better measure of the outcome of the investments.

我還寫了另一個式子當替代方案——這叫幾何平均（geometric mean）。剛剛那是算術平均（arithmetic mean）。幾何平均你可能較不熟，因為概念不同：不是把 n 筆觀測相加，而是把它們相乘，形成乘積，然後不是除以 n，而是取這個乘積的 n 次方根。這個公式常用來估計投資組合的平均報酬，此時 x 要用總報酬，而不是單純報酬。幾何平均只有在所有 x 都非負時才有意義；若塞進負值，乘積可能變負，再開 n 次方根可能變成虛數，所以我們忘掉那種情況——有任何負數就不要套這公式。但它很常用，我也建議用來評估投資；若用總報酬，它更能衡量投資結果。
